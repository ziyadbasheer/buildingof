Challenges & Learnings of Building

This is an introductory article intended to welcome you and provide a taste of what stories on Building of are going to be like. I hope you find it enjoyable.

We've all read a lot about releasing landing pages and MVPs to base subsequent iterations on. But I didn't fully understand what that meant until I launched something. After all, it is my project, over which I have absolute control, and I'm free to conduct it however I wish. Consequently, I'm more likely to build it as I've envisioned, perhaps against what would lead to greater adoption of the project.

Below is an excerpt from an article I've written about succumbing to evolutionary pressure when iterating in the past:

“I wanted to develop something on my own terms. I had a vision and wish to execute on it. I didn't care for other users. I didn't wish for my vision to be tainted by user feedback, I wish to develop based on my ideals. (That's why I developed it as a project), as had it been started as a startup, the market would have forced it to adapt (morph into what would serve users better) or perish.”
Ziyad Basheer—Projects vs Startups

This is further amplified by people's tendency to believe that they're creative geniuses, which is often nothing but misplaced conviction. I suspect that this is a significant contributor to startup failure.

In essence, I've learned that starting off with your mind obstinately set on developing something in a particular fashion might not be wisest approach.

In Context

I started this project with a set of assumptions: it was going to be a blog. However, based on the landing page that I've released at http://buildingof.com, everyone assumed that it was going to be a newsletter, which I suppose is a valid inference.

This led me to explore options I had not initially considered on my own., as I was concerned about hosting—an EC2 instance, or a traditional dedicated server? Managed hosting or roll my own? What about the platform? was I going to write a custom CMS myself? Or use an existing solution? After hints that it was a newsletter, I made it such, which alleviated all of these concerns.

However, developer judgment is required. As the fact that the community suggests that it ought to be a newsletter doesn't necessarily mean that it would work better if it were. It is nevertheless an option worth considering and acting upon if appropriate.

It might seem really obvious that it could've been a newsletter, and perhaps it would've been had I given it more thought, but as previously mentioned I was pursuing what I had envisioned.
