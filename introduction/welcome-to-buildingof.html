Challenges & Learnings of Building

This is an introductory article intended to welcome you and provide a taste of what stories on Building of are going to be like.

We've all read a lot about releasing landing pages and MVPs to solicit user feedback and base subsequent iterations upon. But I didn't fully understand how significant doing so was until I launched something. As after all, it is my project which I have absolute control over, and I'm free to conduct it however I wish. Consequently, I'm more likely to build it as I've envisioned it, perhaps against what would lead to greater adoption of the project.

Below is an excerpt from an article I've written in the past about succumbing to evolutionary pressure when iterating:

“I wanted to develop something on my own terms. I had a vision and wish to execute on it. I didn't care for other users. I didn't wish for my vision to be tainted by user feedback, I wanted to develop based on my ideals. (That's why I developed it as a project as opposed to a startup in pursuit of commercial success), as had it been started as a startup, the market would have forced it to adapt (morph into what would serve users better) or perish.”
Projects vs Startups—Ziyad Basheer

In essence, I've learned that starting off with your mind obstinately set on developing something without in a particular fashion might not be wisest approach.

In Context

I started Building of with a set of assumptions, one of which was that it was going to be a blog. However, based on the landing page that I've released at Building of and feedback I've received, everyone assumed that it was going to be a newsletter, which I suppose is a valid conclusion to reach based on the information that I've made available.

This led me to explore options I had not initially considered on my own., as I was concerned about hosting—an EC2 instance, or a traditional dedicated server? Managed hosting or roll my own? What about the platform? was I going to write a custom CMS myself? Or use an existing solution? After hints that it could be a newsletter, I considered making it such, which would've alleviated all of those concerns.

However, judgment is required in the face of opinion. As the fact that the community suggests that it ought to be a newsletter doesn't necessarily mean that it would work better if it were. It is nevertheless an option worth considering and acting upon if appropriate.

It might seem really obvious that it could've been a newsletter, and perhaps it would've been had I given it more thought, but as previously mentioned, I was pursuing what I had envisioned.
